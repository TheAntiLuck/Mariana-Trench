The location of the Mariana Trench.

The trench is located in the North Western Pacific Ocean, in between Japan, the Philippenes, and Australia.

Right above the trench and directly east is Guam, the largest of the volcanic islands, followed by the Mariana Islands.

Plates and Boundaries

There are 3 Oceanic plates at work here: The Philippenes minor plate, the Mariana micro plate, and the Oceanic major plate.

Between the Philippene Plate and the Mariana plate is a convergent plate boundary (plates are coming together), which make volcanic islands.

Between the Mariana Plate and the Pacific Plate is a divergent plate boundary (plates are pulling apart), which make a mid-ocean-ridge.

The formation of the Mariana Trench.

Even though there are multiple plate boundaries at work, the convergent plate boundaries remain dominant.

The Mariana Trench was formed by the collision of the Philippene Plate and the Pacific Plate.

The Pacific Plate is more dense and subducts beneath the mantle of the Philippenes Plate.
